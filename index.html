**🚲 Bicycles**

1.  **Types of Bicycles**

    *   Road Bikes: Light and fast on roads, with skinny tires.
    *   Mountain Bikes: Tough bikes with bouncy bits for dirt trails.
    *   Hybrid Bikes: Good all-rounders for streets and easy trails.
    *   Cruiser Bikes: Chill, comfy bikes for just riding around.
    *   Folding Bikes: Small, easy to carry, great for city life.
    *   Fixed-Gear/Single-Speed: Simple bikes that are easy to keep running.
    *   Electric Bikes (E-Bikes): Bikes with a battery to give you a boost.
2.  **Parts of a Bicycle**

    *   Frame
    *   Fork
    *   Handlebars
    *   Brakes (rim, disc, which can be hydraulic)
    *   Wheels and tires
    *   Gears (chain, cassette, derailleur)
    *   Saddle (seat)
    *   Pedals
    *   Bouncy bits (front, back, or both)
3.  **Basic Upkeep**

    *   Oil your chain often.
    *   Pump up your tires to the right amount.
    *   Adjust your brakes and gears.
    *   Clean the bike when it gets muddy.
    *   Tighten any loose bolts.

**🛵 Scooters**

1.  **Types of Scooters**

    *   Kick Scooters: You push them; they fold up and are light.
    *   Electric Scooters (E-Scooters): Battery-powered, you twist the throttle to go.
    *   Mobility Scooters: For people who have trouble walking, usually with three or four wheels.
    *   Gas Scooters: Use gas to run.
2.  **Important Things**

    *   Deck (where you stand)
    *   Handlebar (Folds down sometimes)
    *   Battery and motor (if it's electric)
    *   Throttle and brakes
    *   Bouncy bits
    *   Lights (if it's street legal)
3.  **Be Safe**

    *   Watch the traffic and follow rules.
    *   Wear a helmet.
    *   Use lights when it's dark.
    *   Don't go where it's too crowded.
    *   Check your brakes and tires a lot.

**🪖 Helmets**

1.  **Types of Helmets**

    *   Bike Helmets: Light, with holes for air, made to save your head.
    *   Motorcycle Helmets: Heavier, some cover your whole face, some don't.
    *   Scooter Helmets: Like motorcycle helmets, but maybe lighter.
    *   Skating/BMX Helmets: Cover more of your head.
    *   Smart Helmets: Have lights, speakers, or Bluetooth.
2.  **What's in a Helmet**

    *   Hard outer shell (to spread the hit)
    *   Foam inside (to cushion the hit)
    *   Strap (to keep it on)
    *   Holes for air
    *   MIPS (helps with angled hits)
3.  **Keep Safe**

    *   Always buckle the strap.
    *   Get a new helmet after a crash or every 5 years.
    *   Make sure it fits right.
    *   Check for safety stickers.

🧰 2. Bike Stuff You Need

Lights (white in front, red in back – gotta have 'em at night)

Bell or horn (most places want you to have one)

Lock (U-lock, chain, or folding)

Fenders to keep the mud off

Bags and racks for stuff

Speedometer or GPS

Water bottle holder

Fix-a-flat kit and pump

Mirrors for your helmet or handlebars

⚙️ 3. Taking Care of Your Bike - Quick Tips
What to Do How Often
Clean and oil chain Every 100–150 km
Pump up tires Once a week
Check brakes Once a month
Fix gears If they're not shifting smooth
Change chain Every 2000–4000 km
Fix wobbly wheels When they're wobbly
🛴 SCOOTERS (Explained)
🧍 1. Kick Scooters

Light, you push with your foot

Good for short hops

Kids love 'em, good for that last bit of a trip

Folds up to carry
⚡ 2. Electric Scooters
What's Inside:

Motor: Usually in the wheel; from 250W to 2000W

Battery: Usually inside; can take it out or not

Throttle: How you go; thumb or twist

Brakes: Electric, disc, drum, or your foot

Suspension: Makes it smoother; front, back, or both

How Far: 15 to 100+ km

How Fast: Usually 25–45 km/h

Charging It:

Use the charger it came with

Plug it in after every ride, or when it's low

Takes 3–8 hours to fill up

🛠️ Scooter Care:

Keep tires pumped up (if they're air-filled)

Look at brakes and cables

Wipe it down

Tighten everything up

Keep the battery dry

Covers your forehead (one or two fingers above your eyebrows)

Straps make a “V” shape by your ears

Doesn't wiggle when you shake your head

Feels good, but not too loose
